Longitudinal Summary

Over the reported periods in 2021, research and development (R&D) expenses showed a significant increase, with Q1 2021 R&D expense at $666 million, more than doubling from $324 million in Q1 2020. The company consistently recorded write-downs related to cost of revenues and R&D expenses each quarter, ranging from $35 million to $36 million per quarter, accumulating to $106 million by Q3 2021. Cash flow statements repeatedly emphasized that cash generated from operations funded ongoing R&D projects, including proprietary battery cells and manufacturing ramp-ups at multiple Gigafactories. Management commentary in Q4 2021 indicated a conceptual shift in how they view R&D versus product development, suggesting some integration or overlap. Stock-based compensation was also a recurring component of R&D expenses, recorded alongside other operating expenses.
