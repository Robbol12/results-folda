[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: net income

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, Tesla's net income showed a strong upward trajectory, with significant increases reported each quarter and culminating in a full-year GAAP net income of $5.52 billion. Management highlighted improvements in auto gross margin excluding credits and record production and deliveries as key drivers. The net income attributable to common stockholders rose from $438 million in Q1 to $1.62 billion in Q3, reflecting favorable changes compared to the prior year periods. The company also noted positive changes in net operating assets and liabilities and non-cash expenses and gains as contributors to net income growth. By year-end, Tesla's accumulated profitability since inception turned positive, underscoring a milestone in the company's financial performance.

Forecasting credibility: None

Consequently, it is not possible to assess forecasting credibility for net income based on the available data. Any commentary on net income was retrospective or descriptive rather than predictive, so no credibility rating can be assigned.

Key concerns:

  - Some discrepancies between transcript statements and filing figures (e.g., non-GAAP net income references) require careful interpretation.
  - Noncontrolling interests and redeemable noncontrolling interests showed variable impacts but were generally immaterial.

Conclusion:
Tesla's net income in 2021 demonstrated robust growth, with GAAP net income attributable to common stockholders reaching $5.52 billion for the year, a substantial increase from prior periods. This growth was driven by operational improvements, including higher auto gross margins excluding credits and favorable changes in net operating assets and liabilities. Despite the absence of forward guidance on net income, the consistent upward trend and positive accumulated profitability indicate strong financial performance. However, without explicit guidance-actual comparisons, the reliability of management's forecasting on net income cannot be assessed.
