[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: net income

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, Tesla's net income showed a strong upward trajectory, with each quarter reporting significant increases compared to the prior year. Management highlighted record production and deliveries as key drivers behind the growth in net income, culminating in a full-year GAAP net income of $5.52 billion. The filings consistently reported net income attributable to common stockholders rising from $438 million in Q1 to $1.62 billion in Q3, and finally to $5.52 billion for the full year. Noncontrolling interests had minor fluctuations but were generally immaterial to the overall net income picture. Management emphasized the milestone of accumulated profitability becoming positive by year-end 2021.

Forecasting credibility: None
No forward guidance or explicit forecasts for net income were provided by management during the periods reviewed, so it is not possible to assess forecasting credibility for this topic. The absence of guidance-actual pairs means no pattern of met, exceeded, or missed forecasts can be established. Therefore, the credibility rating must remain null.

Trends:
  - Strong sequential net income growth
  - Record production and deliveries driving profitability
  - Increasing GAAP net income milestones
  - Minor fluctuations in noncontrolling interests
  - Positive accumulated profitability by year-end

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Dependence on production and delivery volumes
  - Impact of non-cash expenses and gains
  - Foreign currency exchange rate fluctuations
  - Mark-to-market remeasurement effects

Key concerns:
  - No forward-looking net income guidance provided, limiting forecasting assessment.
  - Some discrepancies between transcript statements and filing figures on non-GAAP vs GAAP net income milestones require careful interpretation.
  - Noncontrolling interests showed minor variability but were generally immaterial.

Conclusion:
Tesla's net income in 2021 demonstrated robust growth, driven primarily by record production and deliveries, and supported by favorable changes in net income excluding non-cash expenses and gains. The company achieved a significant milestone of $5.52 billion in GAAP net income for the year, turning accumulated profitability positive since inception. Despite the strong results, management did not provide forward guidance on net income, so forecasting credibility cannot be evaluated. Overall, the evidence indicates a solid and improving net income performance throughout 2021.
