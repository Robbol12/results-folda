[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: revenue

Quarters searched: 4

Over the course of 2025, total revenues showed some fluctuations with a general trend of decline in the first half of the year followed by partial recovery in the third quarter and strong growth by year-end. Automotive regulatory credits and energy generation and storage sales exhibited variability quarter-to-quarter, with energy sales generally increasing. Services and other revenue steadily increased throughout the year. Management highlighted record revenues and gross profits in later quarters, particularly in energy, while filings detailed revenue breakdowns and noted changes in R&D expenses as a percentage of revenue.

Forecasting credibility: None
No forward guidance paired with actual revenue results was provided in the evidence, so it is not possible to assess management's forecasting credibility for revenue. Without any guidance-actual pairs, no pattern of meeting or missing estimates can be established.

Trends:
  - Declining total revenues in early 2025
  - Increasing energy generation and storage sales
  - Rising services and other revenue
  - Volatile automotive regulatory credits
  - Year-end revenue growth and record gross profits

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Revenue recognition under ASC 606
  - R&D expenses as a percentage of revenue increasing
  - Variability in automotive regulatory credits
  - Impact of warranty and cost of services on revenue

Key concerns:
  - No forward revenue guidance was provided, limiting ability to judge forecasting accuracy.
  - R&D expenses as a percentage of revenue increased notably, which may pressure margins.
  - Automotive regulatory credits showed significant quarter-to-quarter volatility.

Conclusion:
Revenue experienced a mixed trajectory in 2025, with total revenues decreasing in the first half of the year but rebounding in the third quarter and achieving strong year-end growth, particularly driven by energy generation and storage sales and services. Automotive sales revenue declined early but remained a significant portion of total revenue. The company follows ASC 606 for revenue recognition, including for automotive sales and services, and notes warranty expenses as part of cost of revenues. Despite the absence of forward guidance, management emphasized record financial metrics in later quarters, indicating confidence in revenue performance. Overall, revenue trends suggest a recovery and growth phase by the end of 2025, though variability in certain revenue streams and rising R&D expense ratios warrant monitoring.

[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: margins

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 92

Over the course of 2025, margins showed a mixed but generally improving trend. Early in the year, automotive margins declined sequentially due to lower deliveries, reduced fixed cost absorption, and lower regulatory credit revenues, while services and other margins were pressured by used car and insurance businesses. Mid-year, margins in services, energy generation, and solar improved sequentially due to higher profits and ramped deployments. By Q3, total automotive and services gross margins declined year-over-year, but automotive margins excluding credits increased slightly. In Q4, automotive margins excluding credits improved significantly sequentially, supported by better supercharging business margins.

Forecasting credibility: None
No forward guidance paired with actual results on margins was provided by management during the periods reviewed. Therefore, it is not possible to assess forecasting credibility for margins. Any commentary on margin trends is retrospective and does not include explicit forecasts to compare against actual outcomes.

Trends:
  - early year automotive margin decline
  - mid-year services and energy margin improvement
  - year-over-year automotive and services margin decline by Q3
  - Q4 sequential automotive margin improvement excluding credits

Recurring themes/risks:
  - impact of delivery volumes on automotive margins
  - regulatory credit revenue fluctuations
  - used car and insurance business pressures
  - fixed cost absorption variability
  - supercharging business margin contribution

Key concerns:
  - No forward guidance on margins limits ability to evaluate management's forecasting accuracy.
  - Year-over-year decline in total automotive and services gross margin despite some automotive margin improvement excluding credits suggests offsetting pressures.
  - Sequential margin improvements appear linked to specific business segments rather than broad-based margin expansion.

Conclusion:
Margins in 2025 experienced volatility driven by operational factors such as delivery volumes, regulatory credits, and segment-specific performance. Automotive margins excluding credits showed resilience and improvement by year-end, while total automotive and services margins declined year-over-year, indicating mixed underlying dynamics. Services and other margins fluctuated with used car, insurance, and supercharging business performance. The absence of forward guidance on margins means these observations are descriptive rather than predictive, highlighting the importance of monitoring segment-level drivers to understand margin trends.
