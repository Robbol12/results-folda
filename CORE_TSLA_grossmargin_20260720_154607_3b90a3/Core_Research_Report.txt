[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, gross margin trends showed a clear improvement in the automotive segment, with management expressing optimism about margin strength despite some headwinds, particularly in the energy generation and storage segment. Automotive gross margins steadily increased from around 26% in Q1 to over 29% by year-end, driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix improvements. Energy generation and storage margins remained negative or low due to product ramp challenges and manufacturing underutilization. Management highlighted the potential for further margin upside from software offerings and new product ramps. The guidance provided was generally optimistic and aligned with the actual results reported in filings.

Forecasting credibility: High
There is one explicit guidance/actual pair with a verdict, where management's forecast of automotive gross margin improvement was met in Q4 2021, with actual automotive gross margin excluding credits reaching 29.2%, the highest yet. Across the periods, management's optimistic tone about margin improvements was consistent with the actual reported increases in automotive gross margin. The filings provide precise figures that confirm management's commentary, supporting a high credibility rating for their forecasting on gross margin.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin steadily increasing
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin declining or negative
  - Improvement in services and other segment margins
  - Positive impact from factory utilization and cost reductions
  - Software and hardware mix as future margin drivers

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Manufacturing underutilization during product ramp
  - Negative margins in energy storage and Solar Roof
  - Impact of regulatory credits on automotive margins
  - Product mix shifts affecting segment margins

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage segment gross margin remained negative or declined due to ramp challenges and underutilization, which could pressure overall profitability.
  - The negative gross margin in energy storage and Solar Roof segments contrasts with strong automotive margins, indicating uneven performance across business lines.
  - No accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across filings; figures are consistent and comparable.

Conclusion:
Gross margin in the automotive segment showed a clear and consistent upward trajectory throughout 2021, improving from approximately 26% in Q1 to nearly 29.3% by year-end, driven by cost efficiencies, increased production, and favorable product mix. In contrast, the energy generation and storage segment experienced negative or declining gross margins due to product ramp challenges and manufacturing underutilization, particularly with Solar Roof and Megapack products. Management's guidance on gross margin strength was met or exceeded, especially in automotive, supporting confidence in their forecasting. Overall, the company's gross margin performance reflects strong execution in automotive while highlighting ongoing challenges in the energy segment.
