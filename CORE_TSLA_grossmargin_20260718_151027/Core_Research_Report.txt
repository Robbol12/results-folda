[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 97

Over the reported periods in 2022, gross margin for the automotive segment showed variability, with a peak in Q1 at 32.9% followed by declines in subsequent quarters to 27.9% in Q2 and Q3, and a slight recovery to 28.5% for the full year. Management highlighted record gross margins early in the year but acknowledged sequential declines and the impact of price cuts later in the year. The energy generation and storage segment showed improvement, moving from negative gross margins early in the year to positive 7.4% by year-end. Services and other segment margins also improved modestly, partially offsetting automotive margin declines. Management's guidance was limited but included a forecast of automotive gross margin above 20% after price cuts, which was consistent with the actual year-end figure of 28.5%.

Forecasting credibility: High
There is one explicit guidance/actual pair regarding automotive gross margin in Q4 2022 where management forecasted a margin above 20%, and the actual reported margin was 28.5%, exceeding the guidance threshold. This indicates management's forecasting on gross margin was credible in the only available pair. The actual figures from filings provide reliable confirmation of management's statements.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin peaked in Q1 2022
  - Sequential decline in automotive gross margin through Q2 and Q3
  - Slight recovery in automotive gross margin by year-end
  - Energy segment gross margin improved from negative to positive
  - Services and other segment gross margin modestly improved

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Impact of inefficiencies on gross margin
  - Effect of price cuts on automotive gross margin
  - Regulatory credits influence on reported margins
  - Operating leverage improvements
  - Energy segment cost reductions

Key concerns:
  - No explicit guidance/actual pairs for most quarters limit assessment of forecasting consistency.
  - Automotive gross margin volatility suggests sensitivity to operational and pricing factors.
  - Energy segment gross margin improvements may reflect changes in product mix or cost structure.

Conclusion:
Gross margin for the automotive segment showed strong performance early in 2022 but faced pressure in subsequent quarters, likely due to operational inefficiencies and price reductions. Despite these challenges, management's guidance for Q4 2022 gross margin above 20% was met and exceeded, with a reported 28.5% margin for the full year. The energy generation and storage segment improved significantly, turning positive by year-end, which partially offset automotive margin declines in the total automotive & services segment. Overall, gross margin trends reflect a dynamic operational environment with management demonstrating credible forecasting in the limited guidance provided.
