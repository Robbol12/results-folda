TSLA: has management accurately forecasted gross margins

12 quarter(s) searched · 113 filing section(s) searched · 12 period(s) with evidence

Longitudinal Summary

Over the period, management's forecasting of gross margins showed a mix of hits and misses. Initially, they exceeded expectations in Q1 2022, but then missed in Q2 2022. They met expectations in Q1 2023 but missed again in Q1 2024. The trend was generally a decrease in gross margins over time.

Forecasting Credibility: Medium

Management's forecasting credibility is rated as Medium due to a mix of hits and misses in predicting gross margins over the period.

Trends

  • Decrease in gross margins over time

Recurring Themes / Risks

  • Impact of inefficiencies on margins
  • Challenges from ramp inefficiencies
  • Impact of pricing actions on margins

Key Concerns

  • Management's inability to consistently forecast gross margins accurately
  • Potential impact of ongoing challenges on future margins

Conclusion

Based on the evidence provided, management's forecasting of gross margins has been inconsistent, with a mix of hits and misses. While they exceeded expectations at times, there were also instances of missing the forecast. The overall trend of decreasing gross margins over time raises concerns about the company's ability to maintain healthy margins in the future.
