[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross profit

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, gross profit and gross margin showed a consistent upward trend, particularly in the automotive segment. Management expressed optimism early in the year about resolving headwinds and improving gross margin strength. Throughout the quarters, automotive gross margin excluding credits steadily increased, reaching record highs by Q4 2021. The energy segment, however, faced challenges with negative gross margins persisting and declining due to product mix and ramp-up inefficiencies. The filings corroborate management's commentary, showing a rise in automotive gross margin from around 25-26% in early 2021 to nearly 29-30% by year-end.

Forecasting credibility: High
There is one explicit guidance/actual pair with a verdict, where management forecasted an increase in Model S gross margin with higher production and Model X ramp, which was met as automotive gross margins reached record highs by Q3 and Q4. Across the year, management's optimistic guidance about gross margin strength and improvement was consistently supported by actual results reported in filings and transcripts. This pattern of met or exceeded expectations supports a high credibility rating for management's forecasting on gross profit.

Trends:
  - Increasing automotive gross margin
  - Negative and declining energy gross margin
  - Improvement driven by cost reduction and Shanghai factory utilization
  - Record automotive gross margins by Q4 2021

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Energy segment margin pressure due to product mix and ramp inefficiencies
  - Impact of regulatory credits on reported margins
  - Importance of production ramp and factory utilization

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin declined from 0.9% in 2020 to -4.6% in 2021 due to Solar Roof ramp and Powerpack phase-out, indicating margin pressure in this segment.
  - No accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across filings; however, the negative margins in energy contrast with strong automotive performance, highlighting segment risk.

Conclusion:
Gross profit in 2021 showed a clear improvement trend, primarily driven by the automotive segment's increasing gross margins, which rose from approximately 25-26% early in the year to nearly 29-30% by year-end. Management's guidance about margin strength and increases, especially related to Model S and Model X production ramps, was met or exceeded, confirming their forecasting reliability. Conversely, the energy segment experienced margin declines due to product mix shifts and manufacturing underutilization during new product ramps. Overall, the company's gross profit performance was strong and consistent with management's optimistic outlook, supported by detailed filing data.
