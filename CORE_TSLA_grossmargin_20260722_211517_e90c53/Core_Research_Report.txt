[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, gross margin trends showed a clear improvement in the automotive segment, with management expressing optimism about margin strength despite some headwinds, particularly in the energy generation and storage segment. Automotive gross margins steadily increased from around 26% in Q1 to over 29% by year-end, driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix improvements. Energy generation and storage margins remained negative or low due to product ramp challenges and manufacturing underutilization. Management highlighted the potential for further margin expansion, especially as software-based revenue grows. The reported actuals consistently confirmed management's optimistic outlook for automotive margins, while energy margins lagged.

Forecasting credibility: High
Management's guidance on gross margin strength and improvement was consistently met or exceeded across the periods with paired guidance and actuals. The Q4 2021 guidance explicitly forecasted optimism on margin expansion, which was met with a reported automotive gross margin increase from 25.6% to 29.3%. The consistency between management commentary and filing-reported actuals supports a high credibility rating for their forecasting on gross margins.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin steadily increasing
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin declining or negative
  - Improvement driven by cost reduction and factory utilization
  - Growing contribution from software and hardware mix

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Manufacturing underutilization impacting energy margins
  - Product ramp challenges in Solar Roof and Megapack
  - Uncertainty in near-term gross margin fluctuations
  - Potential upside from software-based revenue

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage segment consistently shows negative or declining gross margins due to product ramp and manufacturing inefficiencies.
  - Temporary manufacturing underutilization and phasing out of older products like Powerpack affect margin comparability across periods.

Conclusion:
Gross margin performance in 2021 was characterized by strong and consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with reported automotive gross margins rising from approximately 26% in Q1 to 29.3% for the full year, confirming management's optimistic guidance. This improvement was driven by cost reductions, increased factory utilization, and favorable product mix shifts. Conversely, the energy generation and storage segment experienced declining gross margins, falling from 0.9% in 2020 to -4.6% in 2021, primarily due to higher Solar Roof proportion and manufacturing challenges. Overall, the company's gross margin trajectory reflects solid operational execution in automotive, offset by ongoing challenges in energy products.
