[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, gross margin trends showed a clear improvement in the automotive segment, with automotive gross margins increasing steadily from around 26% in Q1 to over 29% by year-end. Energy generation and storage gross margins remained challenging, with negative or near-zero margins primarily due to the Solar Roof product ramp and manufacturing underutilization. Management consistently expressed optimism about gross margin strength improving as headwinds resolved, particularly in automotive, which was supported by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product scaling. The energy segment's gross margin challenges persisted throughout the year, though some improvements were noted in energy storage. Overall, the company met or exceeded its automotive gross margin guidance, while energy margins remained a known area of difficulty.

Forecasting credibility: High
Management's guidance on gross margins, especially in the automotive segment, was consistently optimistic and was met or exceeded in actual results across the periods. The three guidance/actual pairs available all resolved positively, with actual automotive gross margins increasing as forecasted. The energy segment's negative margins were transparently disclosed and aligned with management's commentary on headwinds and product ramp challenges. The presence of precise filing-sourced actuals further supports the credibility of management's forecasts.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin steadily increasing
  - Energy gross margin negative or near zero
  - Improvement in automotive cost efficiency
  - Solar Roof manufacturing underutilization impacting energy margins
  - Optimism on margin expansion over longer term

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Solar Roof product ramp challenges
  - Manufacturing underutilization in energy segment
  - Cost reduction efforts in automotive
  - Mix shift towards software and hardware in automotive
  - Energy storage margin improvements partially offsetting negatives

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage gross margins remained negative or very low throughout 2021 due to Solar Roof ramp and service costs, indicating ongoing operational challenges in that segment.
  - No significant accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across filings; however, the negative gross margins in energy segment warrant monitoring for sustainable improvement.

Conclusion:
Gross margin performance in 2021 was characterized by strong and consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with gross margins rising from approximately 26% to over 29% by year-end, driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product scaling. In contrast, the energy generation and storage segment struggled with negative or near-zero gross margins due to the Solar Roof ramp and manufacturing inefficiencies. Management's optimistic guidance on automotive gross margins was met or exceeded, demonstrating credible forecasting in that area. The energy segment remains a key area of concern, but some margin improvements in energy storage partially offset the negatives. Overall, the company's gross margin trajectory is positive, primarily driven by automotive strength.
