Longitudinal Summary

Over the course of 2021, Tesla's net income showed a strong upward trajectory, with record production and deliveries contributing to significant increases in GAAP net income each quarter. The company surpassed $1 billion in net income in Q1 and Q2, and by year-end reported $5.52 billion in net income attributable to common stockholders, marking a substantial improvement over the prior year. The increases were driven primarily by higher net income excluding non-cash expenses and gains, alongside favorable changes in net operating assets and liabilities. Noncontrolling interests showed some fluctuations but generally decreased as a percentage of net income. Management highlighted the milestone of accumulated profitability becoming positive in 2021, underscoring Tesla's transition to sustained profitability.
