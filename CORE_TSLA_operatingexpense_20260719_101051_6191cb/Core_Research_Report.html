Longitudinal Summary

Over the periods from Q1 2020 through Q4 2021, operating expenses showed a general trend of becoming more efficient as a percentage of revenue, driven by operational efficiencies and process automation. Management frequently highlighted the impact of stock-based compensation (SBC), particularly related to the 2018 CEO Performance Award, as a significant non-cash component affecting operating expenses. While operating expenses were expected to decrease relative to revenue, there were periods, notably in Q1 2021 and Q4 2021, where expenses increased due to investments in technology, growth, and one-time SBC-related charges. Despite these fluctuations, management maintained guidance that operating expenses as a percentage of revenue would decline over time with expanding sales and operational improvements. The filings consistently disclosed warranty expenses as part of cost of revenues and noted the non-cash nature of SBC within SG&A expenses.

For example, in Q1 2021 and Q4 2021, management forecasted operating expense trends that did not materialize as expected, with expenses increasing due to investments and SBC impacts contrary to prior expectations of declines. This pattern of missing guidance reduces confidence in management's forecasting credibility on operating expenses. The absence of any met or exceeded guidance pairs further supports a low rating.
