[CORE RESEARCH] DRS: backlog

Quarters searched: 2
Filing sections searched: 70

Over the observed periods from Q1 2022 through Q4 2023, the backlog showed a general trend of growth with some fluctuations. Early 2022 saw modest increases and some decreases in backlog, with a notable decrease of $336 million in Q2 2022 despite bookings exceeding revenue by 15%. From Q4 2022 onward, backlog increased significantly, with large jumps reported each quarter into 2023, driven by new contracts such as the multi-boat contract for the Columbia Class submarine program. By the end of 2023, backlog had more than doubled compared to the start of 2022, reflecting strong demand and contract wins across operating segments.

Forecasting credibility: High
There is one explicit guidance/actual pair with a verdict, where the backlog increase forecasted in Q4 2022 was met in Q1 2023 with an actual increase of $1,277 million to $4,272 million from $2,995 million, matching management's expectations. The consistency of backlog reporting in filings and the alignment of actuals with management commentary support a high credibility rating. The detailed filing data provides reliable and transparent backlog figures, reinforcing confidence in management's forecasting.

Trends:
  - modest backlog growth early 2022
  - backlog decrease mid-2022 despite strong bookings
  - significant backlog growth late 2022 through 2023
  - large contract-driven backlog increases
  - backlog more than doubled by end 2023

Recurring themes/risks:
  - backlog includes funded and unfunded components
  - backlog growth driven by new contracts and demand
  - disposal of certain backlog components (e.g., GES backlog)
  - backlog provides revenue visibility for future years

Key concerns:
  - Some inconsistencies in backlog figures due to disposals (e.g., GES backlog) and segment reporting require careful interpretation.
  - Backlog definitions remain consistent, but the mix of funded vs. unfunded backlog and their recognition timing could affect revenue forecasts.

Conclusion:
The backlog has been a strong and growing indicator of future revenue, with management consistently emphasizing its role in providing revenue visibility. Despite a mid-2022 dip, backlog growth accelerated significantly from late 2022 through 2023, supported by major contract awards and increased demand across segments. The backlog figures reported in filings are detailed and consistent, with management's guidance on backlog increases being met or exceeded, supporting confidence in backlog as a reliable metric. Overall, backlog trends suggest robust future revenue streams and underpin management's positive outlook on top-line growth.
