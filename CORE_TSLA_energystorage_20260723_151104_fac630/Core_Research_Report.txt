[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: energy storage

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Throughout 2021, Tesla emphasized the critical role of lithium-ion batteries, particularly iron phosphate cathode chemistry, in solving the energy storage challenge. Management consistently highlighted strong demand and ambitious growth targets for energy storage deployments, including Powerwall and Megapack products. Actual deployment figures increased steadily each quarter, from 0.445 GWh in Q1 to 3.99 GWh by year-end, though some discrepancies in reported figures suggest variability in milestone timing. Guidance evolved from targeting 100 GWh annualized cell supply to a long-term vision of a terawatt-hour per year energy business, with a clear transition toward iron-based stationary storage systems. Despite some variability in quarterly results, the overall trajectory showed growth and strategic shifts in chemistry and scale.

Forecasting credibility: Medium
Tesla provided two guidance/actual pairs with mixed outcomes: the Q2 2021 target to grow cell supply ahead of 50% year-on-year growth was not fully met as actual deployments were lower than implied, but the Q3 2021 deployment figure of 3.01 GWh met the prior guidance. The presence of both met and missed targets indicates moderate forecasting reliability. The use of filing-sourced actuals lends credibility to the assessment, though variability in deployment timing and milestone recognition introduces some uncertainty.

Trends:
  - Increasing energy storage deployments over 2021
  - Shift toward iron phosphate cathode chemistry
  - Ambitious long-term growth targets
  - Quarterly variability in deployment milestones
  - Integration of energy storage with solar and EV charging

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Cell supply bottlenecks impacting growth
  - Timing variability of Megapack project milestones
  - Transition from nickel to iron-based chemistries
  - High demand for stationary storage products

Key concerns:
  - Discrepancies in deployment figures between quarters suggest timing and milestone recognition issues that complicate forecasting accuracy.
  - The large difference between early 2021 reported deployments (445 GWh in Q1 filing) and subsequent quarterly figures (1.72 GWh in Q2, 3.01 GWh in Q3) indicates possible reporting inconsistencies or typographical errors in filings that require clarification.

Conclusion:
Tesla's energy storage business showed clear growth momentum throughout 2021, supported by management's strategic shift to iron phosphate cathode chemistry and ambitious capacity targets. While actual deployment figures increased steadily, some inconsistencies and timing variability in quarterly results highlight challenges in precise forecasting. Management's guidance was partially met, reflecting moderate credibility in their projections. The company's long-term vision of scaling to a terawatt-hour per year energy storage business aligns with its integrated approach combining solar, storage, and EV charging solutions. Overall, Tesla is positioned to expand its energy storage footprint, though supply chain and milestone timing risks remain key factors to monitor.
