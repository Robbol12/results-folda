[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 97

The gross margin for total automotive showed a consistent improvement over the period, with a notable increase from 26.5% in Q1 2022 to 29.6% in Q3 2022, before a slight decrease to 28.5% in Q4 2022. The energy generation and storage segment also saw a significant improvement in gross margin from -20.4% in Q1 2022 to 7.4% in Q4 2022.

Forecasting credibility: High
Management consistently provided accurate guidance on gross margin performance throughout the year, aligning with the actual reported figures.

Trends:
  - Improvement in gross margin for total automotive over the year
  - Significant improvement in gross margin for energy generation and storage segment

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Focus on operating leverage and efficiency improvements driving gross margin
  - Record volumes in certain product lines positively impacting gross margin

Key concerns:
  - The decrease in gross margin for total automotive in Q4 2022 compared to the previous quarter raises questions about sustainability and potential impacts of price cuts on margins.

Conclusion:
Tesla's gross margin performance showed a positive trend overall, with improvements in the automotive segment and a significant turnaround in the energy generation and storage segment. The consistent achievement of guidance demonstrates management's credibility in forecasting gross margin. However, the slight decrease in Q4 2022 raises concerns about the impact of pricing strategies on future margins and the need for continued efficiency measures to maintain profitability.
