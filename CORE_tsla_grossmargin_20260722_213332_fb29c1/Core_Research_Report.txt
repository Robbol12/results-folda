[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, the company's gross margin showed a clear upward trend, particularly in the automotive segment. Early in the year, automotive gross margins remained relatively stable or slightly improved, while energy generation and storage margins were negative due to product ramp challenges. By mid-year, automotive gross margins increased significantly, reaching record highs by Q3 and Q4, driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix improvements. Energy segment margins remained a challenge throughout the year, with negative margins persisting due to manufacturing underutilization and product transitions. Management expressed optimism about gross margin expansion over the longer term, especially as software revenue grows and hardware efficiencies improve.

Forecasting credibility: High
Management's guidance on gross margin was generally optimistic and aligned with actual results, particularly in the automotive segment. The two guidance/actual pairs available both show management met or exceeded expectations, such as the Q4 2021 automotive gross margin of 29.2% matching the guidance of it being the highest yet. This consistency supports a high rating for forecasting credibility. The use of filing-sourced actuals further strengthens the reliability of the assessment.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin increasing steadily
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin negative and declining
  - Improvement in automotive & services segment margins
  - Cost reduction and factory utilization driving margin expansion
  - Shift towards software revenue as a margin driver

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Negative margins in energy generation and storage due to product ramp and manufacturing underutilization
  - Impact of product mix on overall gross margin
  - Uncertainty in near-term margin fluctuations due to headwinds and tailwinds
  - Transition from hardware to software revenue mix

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage segment consistently reported negative gross margins, indicating ongoing challenges in that business line.
  - Temporary manufacturing underutilization and product ramp issues in Solar Roof and Megapack impacted energy margins negatively.
  - No significant accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across the filings; actuals from filings were consistent with management commentary.

Conclusion:
The company's gross margin performance in 2021 was characterized by strong and consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with automotive gross margins rising from around 26% early in the year to over 29% by year-end. This improvement was driven by cost reductions, increased production efficiency, and favorable product mix changes. Conversely, the energy generation and storage segment struggled with negative gross margins throughout the year due to manufacturing inefficiencies and product transitions. Management's optimistic guidance on gross margin strength was met or exceeded, particularly in automotive, supporting confidence in their forecasting. Overall, the gross margin trajectory reflects a maturing automotive business with improving profitability, while the energy segment remains a key area for margin improvement.
