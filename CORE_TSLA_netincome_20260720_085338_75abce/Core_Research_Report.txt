[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: net income

Quarters searched: 4

Over the course of 2021, Tesla's net income showed a strong upward trajectory, with each quarter reporting significant increases compared to the prior year. The company achieved record production and deliveries, which management linked to improved net income performance. By Q4 2021, Tesla reported a cumulative GAAP net income of $5.52 billion for the year, marking a substantial increase and positive accumulated profitability since inception. The filings consistently detailed net income attributable to common stockholders, with noncontrolling interests having a relatively minor and fluctuating impact. Management highlighted milestones such as surpassing $1 billion in GAAP net income for the first time in Q2 and emphasized the company's transition to sustained profitability by year-end.

Consequently, it is not possible to assess forecasting credibility for this topic based on the available evidence. Any commentary on net income performance was retrospective or descriptive rather than predictive.

Trends:
  - Strong sequential and year-over-year net income growth
  - Record production and deliveries supporting profitability
  - Increasing net income attributable to common stockholders
  - Minor fluctuations in net income attributable to noncontrolling interests
  - Transition to positive accumulated profitability

Recurring themes/risks:

  - Mark-to-market remeasurement of interest rate swaps

Key concerns:
  - No forward-looking net income guidance was provided, limiting the ability to evaluate management's forecasting accuracy for this metric.
  - Some discrepancies exist between transcript statements and filing figures regarding non-GAAP versus GAAP net income milestones, but filings provide consistent GAAP net income data.
  - Noncontrolling interests showed variable impacts but were generally immaterial, which should be monitored for any future changes.

Conclusion:
Tesla's net income in 2021 demonstrated robust growth, with GAAP net income attributable to common stockholders rising from $438 million in Q1 to a full-year total of $5.52 billion. This growth was driven by record production and deliveries, improved operational efficiencies, and favorable changes in non-cash expenses and gains. Despite management's positive commentary on net income milestones, no forward guidance was given, precluding an assessment of forecasting reliability. The filings consistently report net income figures, confirming the company's transition to sustained profitability. Overall, Tesla's net income performance in 2021 reflects strong financial health and operational execution.
