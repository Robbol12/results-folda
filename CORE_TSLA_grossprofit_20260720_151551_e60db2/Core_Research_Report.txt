[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross profit

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over 2021, gross profit trends showed a consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with gross margins increasing steadily each quarter and reaching record highs by year-end. Management expressed optimism about gross margin strength early in the year, despite headwinds in the energy segment, which remained negative or low margin throughout. The automotive gross margin excluding credits rose from around 26% in Q1 to nearly 29.2% by Q4, driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix improvements. Energy generation and storage gross profit remained negative or marginally positive, impacted by product ramp challenges and lower fixed cost absorption. Overall, the company transitioned to positive accumulated profitability by the end of 2021, supported primarily by automotive gross margin gains.

Forecasting credibility: High
There is one explicit guidance/actual pair with a verdict, where management's optimistic outlook on gross margin strength was met by actual results showing improved automotive gross margins and positive progress. The consistent pattern of management commentary forecasting margin improvements, which were subsequently confirmed by filing-sourced actuals, supports a high credibility rating. No evidence of systematic misses or overstatements was found in the provided data.

Trends:
  - Increasing automotive gross margin
  - Negative or low energy segment gross margin
  - Improved cost efficiency and factory utilization
  - Shift towards software and hardware mix in automotive
  - Positive accumulated profitability by year-end

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Energy segment margin headwinds
  - Product ramp challenges in energy business
  - Dependence on automotive segment for profitability
  - Impact of regulatory credits on gross margin
  - Manufacturing underutilization in energy products

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage segment consistently reported negative or very low gross profit, with a notable decline in gross margin from 0.9% in 2020 to -4.6% in 2021, driven by Solar Roof ramp and Powerpack phase-out.
  - No accounting or reporting inconsistencies were evident across filings; however, the energy segment's poor margin performance contrasts sharply with automotive gains, posing a risk to overall gross profit sustainability.

Conclusion:
Gross profit in 2021 was primarily driven by strong and improving automotive segment performance, with gross margins rising from approximately 26% to over 29% by year-end, supported by cost reductions and increased production efficiency. Management's optimistic guidance on gross margin strength was validated by actual results, reflecting credible forecasting. Conversely, the energy generation and storage segment remained a drag on gross profit, with negative margins due to product ramp challenges and manufacturing inefficiencies. Overall, the company achieved positive accumulated profitability, underscoring the automotive segment's critical role in gross profit generation.
