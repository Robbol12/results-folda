Quarters searched: 4

Over the reported periods, management provided some commentary on cost of goods sold (COGS) primarily in relation to vehicle sales volume and efficiency improvements. In Q2 2021, they highlighted a reduction in COGS per vehicle by $5,000 year over year, indicating cost improvements despite inefficiencies. By Q4 2021, filings detailed a significant increase in cost of automotive sales revenue driven by higher vehicle deliveries and adjustments related to sales return reserves on vehicles with resale value guarantees. There were also notes on accounting methods for marketable securities sold, but no forward guidance on COGS was provided throughout the periods.

Forecasting credibility: None
No guidance versus actual pairs were provided for cost of goods sold, making it impossible to assess management's forecasting credibility on this topic. The commentary and filings focus on reported results and accounting treatments rather than forward-looking estimates. Therefore, no credibility rating can be assigned.

Trends:
  - COGS per vehicle decreased year over year in early 2021
  - Overall automotive sales cost increased significantly by end of 2021
  - Adjustments related to sales return reserves impacted COGS
  - Accounting method for marketable securities specified

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Impact of vehicle sales volume on COGS
  - Sales return reserve adjustments affecting reported costs
  - Accounting treatment of marketable securities
  - Cost activation timing with new product launches

Key concerns:
  - No forward guidance on COGS limits ability to evaluate forecasting accuracy
  - Adjustments to sales return reserves introduce variability in reported COGS
  - Accounting disclosures on marketable securities sales may complicate cost analysis

Conclusion:
Cost of goods sold evolved with a focus on vehicle sales volume and efficiency improvements, as evidenced by a $5,000 per vehicle reduction in Q2 2021 and a 65% increase in automotive sales cost by Q4 2021 driven by higher deliveries. Adjustments related to sales return reserves on vehicles with resale value guarantees materially affected reported COGS, increasing it by $286 million in 2021. The company uses specific identification for the cost of available-for-sale marketable securities sold, indicating detailed accounting practices. However, the absence of forward guidance on COGS prevents assessment of management's forecasting reliability, and the variability introduced by reserve adjustments should be monitored as a potential risk factor.
