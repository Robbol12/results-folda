Over the periods from Q1 2021 through Q4 2021, management consistently discussed the development and future potential of Full Self-Driving (FSD) technology, emphasizing ongoing improvements and the eventual widespread adoption of FSD in their vehicle fleet. They highlighted the launch and gradual expected growth of the FSD subscription model but refrained from providing specific forward guidance or quantifiable forecasts. The company maintained that FSD was still in beta, limiting revenue recognition and deferring revenue until functionality is delivered. Throughout, filings reiterated the goal of achieving fully self-driving capability and described the recognition of revenue only upon delivery of functionality.

Forecasting credibility: None
No explicit forward guidance paired with actual results was provided for FSD, making it impossible to assess management's forecasting credibility on this topic. Management commentary was largely qualitative and aspirational without quantifiable targets or subsequent performance comparisons. Therefore, no credibility rating can be assigned based on the available evidence.

  - Ongoing development and improvement of FSD technology
  - Gradual rollout and expected growth of FSD subscription
  - Revenue recognition deferred until delivery of functionality
  - Emphasis on future software-based profits from FSD
  - FSD still in beta and limited release

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Uncertainty in timing and scale of FSD subscription adoption
  - Regulatory scrutiny and public criticism of FSD
  - Revenue recognition contingent on delivery of functionality
  - Beta phase limitations on revenue and customer access

Key concerns:
  - No quantifiable guidance or actual results provided for FSD subscription or revenue, limiting transparency on financial impact.
  - Revenue recognition policies indicate conservative accounting, deferring revenue until functionality delivery, which may delay financial benefits.
  - Management's optimistic commentary contrasts with limited current revenue recognition and beta status, suggesting a gap between expectations and near-term financial realization.

Conclusion:
Management consistently communicated the strategic importance and future potential of FSD technology and subscription services but did not provide specific forward guidance or measurable targets. The company’s accounting approach defers revenue recognition until functionality is delivered, reflecting the beta status and limited availability of FSD features. Without any guidance-actual pairs, it is not possible to evaluate forecasting accuracy or credibility. Overall, while FSD is positioned as a key future growth driver, the lack of quantifiable guidance and current revenue recognition limits the ability to assess near-term financial impact or management’s forecasting reliability on this topic.
