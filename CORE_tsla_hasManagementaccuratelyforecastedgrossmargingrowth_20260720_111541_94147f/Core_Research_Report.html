Longitudinal Summary

Over the periods from Q1 2021 through Q4 2022, management consistently expressed optimism about gross margin growth, particularly in automotive segments, citing factors such as product ramp-ups, cost reductions, and operating leverage improvements. Actual reported gross margins generally showed increases in automotive gross margins year-over-year, with some sequential declines in 2022 attributed to production inefficiencies and price cuts. Energy generation and storage margins remained volatile and often negative, impacted by product mix and manufacturing ramp challenges. Management's guidance about gross margin growth or strength was mostly met or exceeded in the automotive segment, while energy segment margins were more variable.

Forecasting Credibility: High

Management provided three guidance/actual pairs related to gross margin growth or decline, all of which were met or exceeded. For example, the optimistic outlook on automotive gross margin strength in 2021 was supported by actual increases from 25.6% to 29.3% by year-end 2021. Similarly, the forecast of at least a few percent growth in automotive gross margin was realized with increases to 32.9% in Q1 2022. Lastly, the expectation to maintain above 20% automotive gross margin despite price cuts was also met, with actual margins at 28.5% for 2022. This consistent pattern of meeting or exceeding guidance supports a high rating for forecasting credibility.
