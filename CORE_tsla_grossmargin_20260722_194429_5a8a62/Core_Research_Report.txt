[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 8
Filing sections searched: 174

Over the period from Q1 2021 through Q4 2022, gross margin trends showed a general improvement in automotive gross margins, with management frequently highlighting record or near-record margins. Automotive gross margins increased from around 26% in early 2021 to a peak of 32.9% in Q1 2022 before declining somewhat to 28.5% by the end of 2022. Energy generation and storage gross margins were more volatile, initially negative and improving to positive by the end of 2022. Management often linked margin improvements to cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix changes, while noting headwinds such as ramping new products and price cuts. The only explicit guidance/actual pair was in Q4 2021, where management's optimistic guidance on automotive gross margin was met by actual results.

Forecasting credibility: High
There is one explicit guidance/actual pair with a verdict in Q4 2021 where management's optimistic guidance on automotive gross margin was met by actual results, indicating credibility. Throughout the periods, management's commentary on gross margin trends aligned well with the actual reported figures from filings, showing consistent and reliable forecasting. No evidence of systematic misses or overly optimistic guidance was found.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin generally increasing through 2021 into early 2022
  - Peak automotive gross margin in Q1 2022 followed by a decline through 2022
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin initially negative, improving to positive by end 2022
  - Improvement driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix
  - Volatility due to product ramp-up and price cuts

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Impact of product ramp-up and manufacturing underutilization on margins
  - Effect of regulatory credits on reported automotive gross margin
  - Price cuts affecting average selling price and margins
  - Energy segment margin volatility due to product mix and fixed cost absorption

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage segment showed significant margin volatility and negative margins in early periods, indicating risk in that business line.
  - Automotive gross margin declined in the latter half of 2022, partially due to price cuts, which may pressure future profitability.
  - No accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across filings; actuals from filings were consistent with management commentary.

Conclusion:
Gross margin, particularly in the automotive segment, showed a strong upward trajectory through 2021, reaching record levels in early 2022, supported by cost efficiencies and factory utilization. However, margins softened in the latter half of 2022, influenced by price reductions and new factory ramp-ups. The energy generation and storage segment experienced more volatility, with negative margins early on improving to positive by the end of 2022. Management's guidance on gross margin has been credible where explicitly provided, and the overall trend suggests a focus on improving automotive gross margin despite challenges in energy margins and pricing pressures.
