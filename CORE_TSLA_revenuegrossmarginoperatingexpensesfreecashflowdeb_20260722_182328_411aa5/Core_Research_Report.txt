[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, gross margin trends showed consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with management expressing optimism about margin strength despite some headwinds, particularly in the energy generation and storage segment. Automotive gross margins increased steadily from around 26% in Q1 to over 29% by year-end, driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product mix improvements. Energy generation and storage margins remained negative or low due to product ramp challenges and manufacturing underutilization. Management's guidance generally anticipated margin improvements, especially in automotive, which were met or exceeded in reported results.

Forecasting credibility: High
Management provided optimistic guidance on gross margin strength and specific margin improvements, particularly in automotive, which were consistently met or exceeded in actual results. The two guidance/actual pairs with verdicts both show management's forecasts were accurate, reinforcing credibility. The use of precise filing data to confirm these outcomes further supports the reliability of management's forecasting on gross margins.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin steadily increasing
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin declining or negative
  - Improvement driven by cost reduction and factory utilization
  - Product ramp impacting energy margins
  - Increasing software and hardware mix influence

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Manufacturing underutilization in energy segment
  - Product ramp challenges (Solar Roof, Megapack)
  - Mix shift towards software impacting margins
  - Regulatory credits exclusion in margin calculations

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage segment gross margins remain negative or low due to ongoing ramp and manufacturing inefficiencies, which could pressure overall margin expansion.
  - No significant accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across filings; however, the negative margins in energy contrast with automotive improvements, highlighting segment-specific risks.

Conclusion:
Gross margin performance in 2021 was characterized by strong and consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with margins rising from approximately 26% to over 29% by year-end, driven by operational efficiencies and product mix. Management's guidance on margin strength was credible and aligned with actual results, as confirmed by filing data. Conversely, the energy generation and storage segment faced margin headwinds due to product ramp challenges and manufacturing underutilization, resulting in negative or low margins throughout the year. Overall, the company's gross margin trajectory is positive, primarily supported by automotive gains, while energy segment risks remain a concern for margin expansion.
