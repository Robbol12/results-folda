[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: net income

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, Tesla's net income showed a strong upward trajectory, with record production and deliveries contributing to significant profitability improvements each quarter. The company reported net income attributable to common stockholders rising from $438 million in Q1 to $5.52 billion for the full year. Management highlighted milestones such as surpassing $1 billion in quarterly net income and achieving positive accumulated profitability since inception. The increases were primarily driven by improvements in net income excluding non-cash expenses and gains, alongside favorable changes in net operating assets and liabilities. Fluctuations due to currency re-measurements were noted but did not obscure the overall growth trend.

Forecasting credibility: None
No forward guidance or explicit net income forecasts were provided by management during the periods reviewed, so it is not possible to assess forecasting credibility for net income. The analysis is based solely on reported actuals and management commentary without any guidance-actual pairs to evaluate accuracy or reliability.

Trends:
  - Strong net income growth throughout 2021
  - Record quarterly and annual net income milestones
  - Increasing net income excluding non-cash expenses and gains
  - Decreasing net income attributable to noncontrolling interests
  - Fluctuations from currency re-measurements

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Impact of non-cash expenses and gains on net income
  - Currency exchange rate effects on reported net income
  - Changes in net operating assets and liabilities influencing net income

Key concerns:
  - No forward-looking net income guidance limits ability to judge forecasting accuracy.
  - Currency re-measurement gains and losses introduce volatility in net income reporting.
  - Noncontrolling interests' net income impact fluctuates and may affect comparability.

Conclusion:
Tesla's net income demonstrated robust growth in 2021, culminating in a record $5.52 billion annual net income attributable to common stockholders. This growth was supported by operational improvements and favorable changes in net operating assets and liabilities, despite some volatility from currency effects and non-cash items. Management did not provide explicit net income guidance, so forecasting credibility cannot be assessed. Overall, the evidence shows a clear and consistent upward trend in net income, reflecting Tesla's improving profitability and operational scale during the year.
