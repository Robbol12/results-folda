[CORE RESEARCH] TSLA: gross margin

Quarters searched: 4
Filing sections searched: 77

Over the course of 2021, gross margin trends showed a clear improvement in the automotive segment, with automotive gross margins increasing steadily from around 26% in Q1 to over 29% by Q4. Energy generation and storage gross margins remained challenging, with negative or near-zero margins due to factors such as higher Solar Roof proportions, manufacturing underutilization, and increased service costs. Management consistently expressed optimism about automotive gross margin strength and margin expansion over the longer term, while acknowledging near-term headwinds in the energy segment. The automotive segment's margin improvements were driven by cost reductions, factory utilization, and product scaling efficiencies.

Forecasting credibility: High
Management's guidance on gross margin strength and optimism was consistently met or exceeded across the periods with paired guidance and actuals. For example, Q4 2021 guidance about increasing automotive gross margin was met with a reported 29.2% margin, the highest yet. The actual filing data corroborates management's optimistic outlook, showing steady margin improvements. This consistent pattern of meeting or exceeding expectations supports a high credibility rating for management's forecasting on gross margin.

Trends:
  - Automotive gross margin steadily increasing
  - Energy generation and storage gross margin negative or near zero
  - Improvement driven by cost reduction and factory utilization
  - Solar Roof manufacturing underutilization impacting energy margins

Recurring themes/risks:
  - Temporary manufacturing underutilization in Solar Roof
  - Higher service maintenance costs in energy segment
  - Product ramp challenges in energy storage
  - Optimism on automotive margin expansion

Key concerns:
  - Energy generation and storage gross margins remained negative or low throughout 2021 despite some improvements, indicating ongoing operational challenges in that segment.
  - The negative gross margin impact from Solar Roof ramp and manufacturing underutilization is a recurring issue that may affect near-term profitability in energy.
  - No significant accounting or reporting inconsistencies were noted across filings; actuals from filings were consistent with management commentary.

Conclusion:
Gross margin performance in 2021 was characterized by strong and consistent improvement in the automotive segment, with automotive gross margins rising from approximately 26% in early 2021 to over 29% by year-end, driven by cost efficiencies and factory utilization. Conversely, the energy generation and storage segment struggled with negative or near-zero gross margins due to the higher proportion of Solar Roof products, manufacturing underutilization, and increased service costs. Management's optimistic guidance on automotive gross margin expansion was validated by actual results, reflecting credible forecasting. Overall, while automotive gross margins showed robust growth, energy segment margins remain a key area of concern for future profitability.
