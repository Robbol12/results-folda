Longitudinal Summary

Over the 2015 reporting periods, management provided multiple revenue guidance targets, generally projecting growth or stable revenue ranges. Actual results showed mixed performance: while some quarters reported record or increased revenues (e.g., Q3 2015 with $112 million), the full year revenue of $389 million represented a 12% increase but missed earlier raised guidance ranges of $500 million to $520 million. Later guidance for 2016 anticipated a revenue decline of 5% to 10%, reflecting a more cautious outlook. The quarterly revenue figures showed variability, with Q4 2015 revenue declining compared to prior quarters and the previous year.

Forecasting Credibility: Low

Management's revenue guidance was frequently missed or overly optimistic relative to actual reported figures. For example, the full year 2015 revenue guidance was raised to $500 million to $520 million, but actual revenue was $389 million, missing the target by a wide margin. Although some quarterly guidance was met or slightly exceeded, the overall pattern includes significant misses, undermining forecasting credibility.

Conclusion

Revenue guidance throughout 2015 was initially optimistic, with management raising full year targets and projecting growth. However, actual revenue fell short of these targets, with the full year 2015 revenue reported at $389 million, missing the raised guidance range of $500 million to $520 million. Quarterly revenues showed some growth, notably a record $112 million in Q3, but Q4 revenue declined both sequentially and year-over-year. For 2016, management forecasted a revenue decline of 5% to 10%, reflecting a more cautious stance. Overall, the evidence indicates that while revenue grew year-over-year, management's revenue forecasts lacked accuracy and reliability.
